Introduction and Application

In 2023, while searching for design-based opportunities, I stumbled upon the FOSS Bitcoin Grant Initiative by Spiral.xyz. I had previously heard of Spiral.xyz in 2022 when Jack Dorsey shared a PDF link on Twitter of the Bitcoin Whitepaper by Satoshi Nakamoto. This recognition and rapport with Cash App and Spiral gave me confidence that the opportunities would genuinely help me achieve my goals. After reviewing all the available opportunities, I chose to pursue the Summer of Bitcoin program due to its focus on Open-Source Design, a novel concept for me. I followed the program’s Twitter account, received notifications of open applications, and submitted my application.


Bootcamp and Proposal

Upon receiving confirmation that I would be progressing through the program, I eagerly anticipated learning from other "Bitcoiners". During the bootcamp, we were required to complete reading assignments that enhanced our conceptual understanding of Bitcoin and blockchain concepts. Occasionally, we were divided into groups to discuss the assigned chapters, engage in conversations about the covered concepts, and document the outcomes of those discussions. These moments not only provided valuable knowledge but also served as networking opportunities for me. Personally, I found that the bootcamp was particularly beneficial for engineer-based interns, as much of the content was technical or computer-oriented. This emphasis on technical aspects led me to frequently network with my cohort members, seeking to maximize my learning experience from the program. When it came time to write proposals, I decided to pitch to two companies: The Bitcoin Layer and Bitcoin Design. Both companies seemed to align with my personal creative goals of brand and marketing design. Unfortunately, I wasn’t able to secure an opportunity with Bitcoin Design. However, one summer afternoon, I received an unexpected call from a gentleman named Matthew Ball, the Creative Director of The Bitcoin Layer and Lead UX/UI Designer at GE. Matt's experience in corporate spaces aligns with a goal I had of learning how to operate within Coporate Spaces. I’ve always been intrigued by how corporations set objectives and align designers to achieve them. Matthew also wanted to congratulate me on my progress thus far and extend an offer to intern with The Bitcoin Layer. I quickly accepted the offer, as Matt further explained how impressed he was with my proposal, particularly my minimalistic style. Hearing that expression was incredibly affirming for me, as I had some feelings of being misplaced amoung the large amount of engineering interns.


Onboarding

After accepting Matt's offer and completing the required legal documentation with Adi and Summer of Bitcoin it was official — this Summer I’ll be interning for The Bitcoin Layer as their Content Manager, managing and orchestrating social channels and content. Due to a holiday weekend, my onboarding was postponed however that didn't stop me from beginning my own journey. I began combing the various outlets The Bitcoin Layer used for content and curated a brief Brand Guide of-sorts that aggreegated much of the current marketing and brand material.


Mid-Term Evaluation

My day-to-day process included reviewing emails and discord messages from the previous night or weekend if it is a Monday. When it comes to my design process, I tend to go straight to sketching first impressions. Doing so helps me relieve myself of eager ideas and even rid myself of previous bias before proceeding to my next step.

After preliminary sketches are done I usually move forward with market and competitor analysis. Usually this process allows me to critique my sketches to evolve the most promising ideas and communicate them better. After researching other media outlets within the Bitcoin community I was able to notice a trend that transcended all social channels.

Many social channels lack a dedicated stylization for their brand and often lack clear typographic hierarchy leading to poor legibility. To alleviate that concern I created a brief typographic system that will help carry communication internally as well as consumer-facing. I more defined type system that’s also cohesive with the branding also allows the brand appear more premium and reliable which within objective and even company slogan.
